67 - 373: Information Systems Consulting Project Course

The Software Development Project course engages junior-level students in team-based projects within Information Systems, focusing on developing solutions for real-world clients. Students collaborate to design, build, and deliver information system solutions while following a structured software project life cycle approach. The course emphasizes delivering sustainable solutions tailored to clients' objectives, organizational constraints, and capabilities.
Students in the Software Development Project course tackle real-world software challenges under the guidance of faculty advisors. Acting as consultants, teams propose, define, design, and implement information system solutions in partnership with clients. The course emphasizes effective project management, teamwork, and communication skills while delivering tangible outcomes aligned with client needs.
Learning Objectives:
Upon completion of the course, students will demonstrate growth in:
  • Identifying and recommending appropriate solutions for client requirements.
  • Applying skills across key phases of the system development life cycle.
  • Collaborating effectively in small software development teams.
  • Applying practical project management techniques to real projects.
Success in the course is contingent upon partnerships formed with clients. Teams prioritize understanding and addressing client needs, ensuring the delivery of valuable solutions. Client evaluation contributes to the final grade, underscoring the importance of delivering impactful solutions to clients.
