Who is a Community Partner?

Professor Mohammad Aazam: Hello, I'm Professor Mohammad Aazam, and I teach the Information Systems Consulting Project course at Carnegie Mellon University’s Qatar Campus. While my colleague Professor Joseph Mertz leads the course in the Pittsburgh Campus, he also serves as the Program Director for the Information Systems program across both our Qatar and Pittsburgh campuses. What is a community partner? A community partner is an organization that collaborates with our student teams to solve real-world information system challenges. These partners range from nonprofits and government entities to startups and private businesses. Our goal is to create impactful solutions that not only benefit our partners but also provide our students with invaluable real-world experience. Anyone passionate about making a positive impact and facing an information system challenge can join us as a community partner. We're particularly interested in organizations aiming to bridge the digital divide, supporting underprivileged communities, and looking to enhance their technological capabilities.

Professor Julia Poepping: Hello, I'm Julia Poepping, Associate Director of Partnership Development at Carnegie Mellon University's Pittsburgh Campus. I work hand in hand with Lubna Barghouti, the Director of Experiential Learning at Carnegie Mellon University’s Qatar Campus. Our collaboration goes beyond mere consultation. We focus on creating sustainable solutions tailored to fit your organization's needs. By partnering with us, you gain access to innovative solutions, technological expertise, and fresh perspectives. This partnership is an opportunity to tackle your most pressing problems while contributing to the education and growth of our students. Becoming a community partner means you'll engage actively with our student teams, providing them with real-world problems to solve. There is no fee to participate, but your commitment is key. You'll need to meet regularly with your team, provide feedback, and share your expertise. In return, you'll receive innovative solutions and improvements, all tailored to your organization's needs. Joining us is easy: fill out the application form on our website, and we’ll guide you through the rest. Our program has a remarkable success and return rate, with 35% of our partners coming back for more projects. This success stems from our commitment to delivering real value and fostering meaningful partnerships. Don't miss this unique opportunity to transform and grow together. Thank you.

Community Impact

From 2013 to 2023, our impactful collaborations with 140 Community Partners, involving 220 project teams, have generated a total contribution of 137,500 hours to our communities. With each project, students invest 540 hours, guided by 90 hours of faculty advising. Notably, 35% of our partners return, and 8 have partnered with us over 4 times, emphasizing the sustained success and trust in our collaborative initiatives. Together, our collaborative efforts are contributing to a sustained positive influence within the communities we serve.
Our student teams have effectively collaborated with several organizations from startups to government institutions.
